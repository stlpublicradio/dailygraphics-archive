Timeline of the 2010 shooting at ABB

2006

Timothy Hendron, an employee of ABB’s manufacturing facility in St. Louis, joins a federal lawsuit alleging the company had mismanaged its pension funds.

Jan. 6, 2010

Hendron, who had been demoted from a supervisor position when the company eliminated a production shift, purchases a 12-gauge shotgun and a high-powered rifle. He also travels to his family’s lake house near Hermann, Missouri, where he stored weapons he used for target practice and hunting.

Jan. 7, 2010

6:34 a.m.

St. Louis police get a 911 call for “shots fired” at ABB in the Mark Twain/I-70 Industrial neighborhood. Sgt. Byron Harrington is the first officer to respond.

Between 6:30 a.m. and 6:45 a.m.

Hendron kills three people and injures five more.

6:49 a.m.

Sgt. William Kiphart initiates a "Code 1200," calling all available officers to respond to the scene. The first officers enter the building.

Approximately 9:30 a.m.

St. Louis SWAT officers find Hendron dead in an office inside the three-building complex. He had shot himself moments after police arrived on the scene.

3 p.m.

SWAT teams from St. Louis, St. Louis County and federal agencies finish searching the 764,000-square-foot complex and turn it over to homicide investigators.

Jan. 11–13, 2010

Funeral services held for 27-year-old Cory Wilson, 55-year-old Terry Mabry, Sr. and 57-year-old Carlton Carter.

Jan. 14, 2010

Factory reopens.

March 12, 2010

The first lawsuits are filed in connection with the shooting. There would eventually be 23 wrongful-death or personal-injury lawsuits filed between 2010 and 2013. All were settled in 2015.

Nov. 17, 2010

Michael Sack, then the commander of the Bureau of Crimes Against Persons and Property, presents the St. Louis Board of Police Commissioners with a 97-page report outlining the investigation into the incident.

Jan. 5, 2011

A memorial is dedicated to the victims.

April 18, 2012

Police commissioners approve a $200,000 donation from the St. Louis Police Foundation for entry tools, shields, shotguns and tactical rifles. The donation ensures that all officers have equipment needed to respond to active shooters.

November 2017

ABB announces that it will close its St. Louis facility as part of a company reorganization. The plant is shuttered in 2018.
