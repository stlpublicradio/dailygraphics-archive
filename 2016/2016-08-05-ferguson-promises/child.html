Ferguson Promises

Here are five organizations’ commitments to Ferguson, and where they stand.

Centene
Commitment
Status
Give $25 million to a Ferguson Service Center that would employ more than 250.
  • Officially opened center on April 15, 2016. Employs 117 full-time workers as of Aug. 2, and 9 contractors for cafe and security. Almost 90 percent live in North City/County
Other achievements
  • Centene Charitable Foundation is also supporting other initiatives.
Emerson
Commitment
Status
Help create 100 youth jobs in the community through a $750,000 investment
  • Helped create 100 youth jobs in the community through a $750,000 investment in STL Youth Jobs, a program that focuses on long-term success by providing education, job coaching and ongoing employment support even after a participant has secured a job.
  • Additionally, helped create 80 more youth jobs in the community through a $500,000 investment in STL Youth Jobs (71% of the people from this second round of 80 jobs created are still working).
  • Supported Save Our Sons, a north St. Louis County job training and placement program launched by the Urban League of Metropolitan St. Louis with a $200,000 investment.
  • Provided $100,000 for the Urban League Community Empowerment Center, at the site of the former QuikTrip, that will house Save Our Sons along with specialized resources to help meet community needs.
Fund early childhood education programs and possibly create an ece learning resource center
  • Commitment grown to $11.8M and more than 30 programs focusing on early childhood education, youth jobs, scholarships for college, technical/trade careers and business support/training.
  • Invested $264,000 in the Ferguson-Florissant Head Start program allowing for increased enrollment for low-income families (first donation of $137,000 followed by a second donation of $127,000).
  • Invested $77,800 in the New Horizons Early Childhood Center covering operational costs and increased enrollment along with an updated playground, refurbishing of the gym and new technological resources.
Includes STEM scholarship effort ($1.5M over five years for 30 scholarships for under-represented or first generation college students)
  • Harris-Stowe University: $500,000 over five years for North County students’ scholarships and to expand STEM.
  • University of Missouri – St. Louis: $1.5 million over five years for North County students’ scholarships.
  • St. Louis Community College: $1 million for STEM specific scholarships for Florissant Valley students.
Ranken Technical College effort involving $1.75M in scholarships for North County students
  • Ranken Technical College: $1.75 million over five years for scholarships
Other achievements
  • Secondary school support:
    • Cardinal Ritter College Prep: $400,000 over four years for scholarships
    • Lutheran North High School: $400,000
    • Incarnate Word Academy: $200,000
    • North County Christian School: $200,000 Business Support and Training
  • Activated the Neighbors Helping Neighbors program initiative to support local commerce and stabilize those businesses negatively affected
  • Granted aid to 11 local businesses, helping with physical repairs, inventory replacement, website development, legal matters and establishing connections with local business resource and entrepreneurship organizations.
Monsanto
Commitment
Status
$1 million in grants:
  • $500,000 to United Way for Ferguson Fund
  • $200,000 to Urban League's Save Our Sons
  • Reinvest North County $200,000 for immediate assistance to area businesses and support area school districts
  • $100,000 to provide legal assistance to local residents with hardships and Children's Legal Alliance Program
  • All grants paid in full
Other achievements
  • OASIS Institute – Through this $50,000 grant, 140 adult volunteers provide math and science tutoring in 17 Ferguson-Florissant elementary schools during the school day. Monsanto Fund dollars support the development of paired reading curriculum in math and science, along with the purchase of technology to support this curriculum.
  • Challenger Learning Center of St. Louis – Monsanto Fund provides $80,000 in funding for the Sprouting Astronauts program which provides hands-on learning opportunities for all 5th grade students and professional development for all 5th grade teachers in the Ferguson-Florissant School District. The program serves 870 students and 36 teachers.
  • YMCA – The Science Gone Mad programs provides enrichment activities and field trips in science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) to students at Berkeley Middle School in the Ferguson-Florissant School District. Support for this program is funded at about $100,000 per year.
  • Maryville University CREST-M STEM Curriculum – Through the grant amounting to $124,995, the university funded the new program called Children using Robotics for Engineering, Science, Technology and Math (CREST-M). In July 2015, 80 students and 18 teachers from Ferguson-Florissant and Ritenour participated in the two-week program. The funding will help university's educators to create an innovative math curriculum for 3rd and 4th graders using LEGO WeDo robotics.
  • Opera Theatre of Saint Louis’ Artists-in-Training program in 25th year – Thanks to the Monsanto Fund, 23 high school students from across the St. Louis region engaged in weekly college-level vocal coaching from faculty at Southern Illinois University-Edwardsville, Washington University, Webster University, and University of Missouri–St. Louis. Included four students from Ferguson-Florissant high schools.
  • Institute for School Partnership – The Monsanto Fund awarded the Institute for School Partnership (ISP) at Washington University with a $1,935,000 grant, beginning July 1, 2015. Ferguson-Florissant schools are among those piloting the new grades 6-8 curricula, and have been using the existing K-5 curricula. Over the three-year grant period, the ISP will create a hands-on, inquiry and project-based science curriculum for middle school students that integrates elements of science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM).
Reinvest North County Fund
Commitment
Status
Raise money through #FergusonRebuild crowdfunding
  • GoFundMe effort raised $40,920. Overall, the fund has raised more than $900,000 and is still active.
Distribute money to businesses damaged in the area
  • The fund paid $756,000 to businesses and four school districts. Of the 55 businesses that received money, 50 are still open.
Urban League
Commitment
Status
Launch Save our Sons initiative to train 500 unemployed African-American men from Ferguson and North County over 2 years; expand program beyond North County.
  • Trained 250 men at the end of August, plan to reach 500 goal in 2017. Program expanded to men in St. Louis city.
Convert old QuikTrip site into a more than $3M empowerment center
  • After tax credit approval and funding from Salvation Army, Community Empowerment Center construction begins in August. Should be complete in March.
