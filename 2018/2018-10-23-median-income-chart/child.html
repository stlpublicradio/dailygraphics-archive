Black, hispanic families had lower net worths than white families in 2016, despite wealth disparity narrowing

Between 1989 and 2016, white families’ median net worth increased slightly. Hispanic and black families’ median net worth more than doubled, but remained much lower than that of white families. Hispanic families in 2016 had 13 percent of the net worth of white families; for black families, 10 percent.

Notes

The Federal Reserve defines “white” as “non-Hispanic white,” “Hispanic” as Hispanic families of any race, “black” as “non-Hispanic African-American or black,” and “other” as “other nonwhite.” All amounts are adjusted for inflation.

Source: The Demographics of Wealth: 2018 Series, Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis
