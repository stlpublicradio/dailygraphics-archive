McGwire through the years

1997

July 31, 1997 - Mark McGwire is traded from the Oakland Athletics to the St. Louis Cardinals in exchange for relief pitcher T.J. Mathews and minor league pitchers Eric Ludwick and Blake Stein. McGwire would hit 58 home runs that season, three shy of Roger Maris’ record of 61.

1998

August 21, 1998 - Associated Press sports reporter Steve Wilstein writes a story about McGwire’s use of androstenedione, a testosterone precursor that was legal in Major League Baseball, but banned in other sports and the Olympics. McGwire admits to taking the supplement, saying "Everybody I know in the game of baseball uses the same stuff I use."

September 8, 1998 - McGwire hits his 62nd home run of the season, breaking Maris’ 37-year-old single-season record. Nineteen days later, in his final at-bat of the season, McGwire hits his 70th home run to set the new record. Sammy Sosa, of the Chicago Cubs, finishes the year with 66.

1999

June 10, 1999 - Missouri Gov. Mel Carnahan signs a bill naming a portion of Interstate 70 in St. Louis after McGwire.

2000

2001

October 4, 2001 - Barry Bonds of the San Francisco Giants hits his 73rd home run of the year to set the new single-season record. About five weeks later, McGwire announces his plans to retire.

Post-Retirement

March 17, 2005 - In testimony to Congress, McGwire refuses to say whether he took steroids as a player. “My lawyers have advised me that I cannot answer these questions without jeopardizing my friends, my family, or myself,” he says in his opening statement.

October 26, 2009 - Tony La Russa announces that McGwire will join the Cardinals in the 2010 season as the team’s hitting coach.

January 11, 2010 - McGwire admits to using steroids during much of his career, including 1998. Later that year, the Missouri General Assembly passes a bill stripping McGwire’s name from Interstate 70 and replacing it with Mark Twain.
