Millennials and the Great Recession

  • There are 71 million millennials in the United States. They were born between 1981 and 1996 (ages 22 to 37 in 2018).

  • The U.S. economy lost 8 million jobs during the Great Recession, which officially began in December 2007 and ended in June 2009.

  • In 2008, the unemployment rate was nearly 8 percent for college graduates aged 20 to 24. The average graduating senior with student loans had racked up $23,200 of debt.

  • In 2009, 20 percent of employment-seeking college graduates had found a job by the time they received their diploma.

    • Source: Pew Research Center, National Association of Colleges and Employers
