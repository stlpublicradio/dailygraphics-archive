Investigations find students responsible in about half of cases

The University Sexual Assault Investigation Board at Washington University investigates cases after students file a written complaint. The board has completed 39 cases since it was established in January 2013. Most cases take seven to nine months, according to Wash U’s Title IX Office. About 56 percent of students accused were found “responsible.” The university has not released numbers on expulsions, but not all students found responsible were expelled. Student activists say that the process takes too long, has too low a conviction rate and doesn’t enforce strong enough consequences. The Title IX Office says its process is fair and thorough, if slow.

Notes

This graphic uses the university’s language for case outcomes. “Responsible” means the panel ruled against the student accused of sexual assault. “Not Responsible” means the panel ruled against the student who accused another of sexual assault. “Withdrawn” means that the individual who filed the case requested that the investigation cease.
