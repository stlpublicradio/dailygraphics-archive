One baseball’s story

We tracked the journey of one baseball from the time it was put in play at the Cardinals’ August 16 game against the Washington Nationals until it was sold at the Authentics Shop in the right field concourse. In the bottom of the 4th inning, Nationals pitcher Tanner Roark used one ball to face four Cardinals batters: Tyler O’Neill, Marcell Ozuna, Paul DeJong and Kolten Wong. The ball was pitched 11 times, hit twice (both outs caught in the outfield) and thrown four times to first base in pickoff attempts (all unsuccessful). On its 11th trip to the plate, the first pitch to Kolten Wong, the ball hit the dirt.

A new ball is put into play after Tyler O’Neill fouls off a full-count pitch from Tanner Roark. O'Neill walks on the next pitch.

Marcell Ozuna flies out on a 2-1 pitch to center fielder Juan Soto.

Paul DeJong lines out on a 2-2 pitch to right fielder Bryce Harper.

Roark attempts three consecutive pickoffs of O’Neill at first, then throws the first pitch to Kolten Wong in the dirt.

The ball is tossed out of play into the Cardinals dugout, where an authenticator, who has tracked the ball around the field, labels it with a hologram sticker.

The ball is taken to the Authentics Shop in the right field concourse, where it is boxed, priced and labeled with the plays the ball was involved in.
