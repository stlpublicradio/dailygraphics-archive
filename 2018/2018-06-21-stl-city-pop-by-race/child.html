St. Louis’ black population continues falling in 2017

In 2017, black St. Louisans made up 46.1 percent of the city’s population, while nearly 44 percent of its residents were white. The black population has fallen each year since 2010. Other race groups tracked by the census have remained stable or risen slightly.

Notes

The Census counts Hispanic as an ethnicity, distinct from race. Here, black, white and Asian are people who indicated they were non-Hispanic. Hispanic includes people of many racial backgrounds.

Source: U.S. Census Population Estimates Program
