Suspensions Index

We used the formula that the Missouri Attorney General's office uses to calculate traffic stop disparities to find out if black students are suspended more or less than their proportion of the student population. Scroll around or zoom out to see more Missouri districts.

The suspension index is the percentage of suspensions given to black students divided by the percentage of black students in a district. Numbers greater than one mean that the percent of suspensions given to black students is greater than the percentage of black students enrolled in that district. Numbers less than one mean that the percentage of suspensions given to black students is less than the percentage of black students enrolled.

Numbers are from the 2014-2015 school year. Only districts where black students represent more than five percent of the total enrollment are shown.

Source: Missouri Department of Elementary and Secondary Education
