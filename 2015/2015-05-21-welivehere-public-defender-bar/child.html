State spending on public defenders per person living under the poverty level, by year

States spend widely varying amounts on public defenders. We divided the total expense by the number of people living below the federal poverty level, and looked at the results from 2008 and 2012. The amounts range from more than $500 per person to less than $1.

Notes

Data for Pennsylvania, California and Florida was unavailable. The most recent spending data for Oregon was from 2011.
