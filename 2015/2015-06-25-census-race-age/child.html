St. Louis' majority-minority is changing by age groups, the region's not so much

St. Louis city has been majority-minority for a few years — meaning those other than non-Hispanic Whites make up a majority of the population. But the composition by age is changing. For example, about 70 percent of people younger than 20 in the city are minorities — in 2010 that number was nearly 75 percent. By contrast, in 2014 minorities made up almost 52 percent of the elderly in the city, but in 2010 they were only 46.5 percent.

Notes

The region includes Franklin, Jefferson, Lincoln, St. Charles and St. Louis counties as well as St. Louis city in Missouri, as well as St. Clair and Madison counties in Illinois.

Source: U.S. Census population estimates
