Juvenile sentencing timeline

2005

Roper vs. Simmons

The Supreme Court, by a 5-4 majority, outlawed the death penalty for defendants under the age of 18, citing their relative immaturity.

2010

Graham vs. Florida

The Supreme Court, by a 6-3 majority, outlawed future mandatory life-without-parole sentences for defendants under the age of 18 who are convicted of non-homicide crimes

2012

Miller vs. Alabama

The Supreme Court, by a 5-4 majority, outlawed future mandatory life-without-parole sentences for defendants under the age of 18 who are convicted of murder

2016

Montgomery vs. Louisiana

The Supreme Court, by a 6-3 majority, rules that any defendant serving life-without-parole for murders committed when they were under 18 has the right to have that sentence reviewed.
