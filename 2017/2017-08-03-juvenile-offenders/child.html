Who does SB 590 affect?

94 inmates currently in Missouri prisons were sentenced to mandatory life without parole for murders they committed as juveniles (under the age of 18). 62 are not yet eligible for parole.

32 inmates are now eligible for parole under the provision of Senate Bill 590, which says juvenile lifers who have spent at least 25 years behind bars can apply for their release. Of those that are eligible, all but five have had hearings.

23 inmates have had parole hearings. Another 4 inmates have hearings scheduled as of July 31. Of these 27, 24 have not received release dates.

Three inmates have gotten release dates.

  • Bradley Houston will be released on May 27, 2020 after spending 34 years in prison. He was 19 when he was sentenced to life without parole
  • Edward Ramsey will be released December 26, 2021 after spending 30 years in prison. He was 19 when he was sentenced to life without parole.
  • Michael McRoberts will be released on March 1, 2022, after spending 32 years in prison. He was 18 when he was sentenced to life without parole

Source: Missouri Department of Corrections
