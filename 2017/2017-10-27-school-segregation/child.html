School segregation in St. Louis

Most schools in the city have a higher percentage of non-white students than the city’s population.

Each dot on the chart represents one school building in each year shown. The percentage is of non-white students, reported by the Department of Elementary and Secondary education. This includes students reported as Hispanic and multiracial. Because not all students’ races were reported, we removed school buildings for which more than five percent of students’ races could not be determined. The dashed line represents the percentage of non-white city residents aged 5-17, according to the Census.

Notes

School-age population in the city data came from the 1990 and 2000 Census and the 2005, 2010 and 2015 American Community Survey 5-year estimates. DESE redacted data where there were four or fewer students of a race in a school in a year. Not all students’ races were reported.

Source: School data from Missouri Department of Elementary and Secondary Education; Census data from the U.S. Census
