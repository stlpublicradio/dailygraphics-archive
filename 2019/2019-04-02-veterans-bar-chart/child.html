U.S. Veterans in their 50s suffer severe pain more than other age groups

Data from a 2010-2014 National Institutes of Health survey shows that veterans in the United States are more likely than non-veterans to suffer from severe pain. Veterans aged 18-39 were more than twice as likely to have severe pain than non-veterans in the same age group. Veterans aged 50-59 were the most likely of any age group to have severe pain.

Notes

Severe pain is defined as pain that occurs “most days” or “every day” and bothers the individual “a lot,” according to the study.

Source: National Center for Complementary and Integrative Health, National Institutes of Health
