Black-owned businesses twice as likely to be denied loans than white-owned firms

Banks more often deny loan applications from minority-owned small businesses than from those owned by white people, according to U.S. Federal Reserve data. Black-owned small businesses are the most likely group to be turned down for a loan. More than half of their loan applications to banks were denied in 2014, the most recent nationwide data available.

Source: Availability of Credit to Small Businesses, Sept. 2017 | U.S. Federal Reserve
