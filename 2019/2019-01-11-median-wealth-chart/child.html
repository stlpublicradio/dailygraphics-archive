Between 1983 and 2016, black families’ wealth dropped as white and Latino families’ wealth grew

The median black family owned 2 percent of the wealth of the median white family in 2016, according to data from the Federal Reserve Board. The median wealth of black families has decreased by more than half since 1983. Black and Latino families are more likely to have zero or negative wealth because of debt.

Notes

Numbers reflect median wealth. According to the Federal Reserve, “Wealth in the United States is measured in terms of net worth, which is the sum of all assets, including the market value of real estate, like a home, minus all liabilities.”

Source: Federal Reserve Board Survey of Consumer Finances
