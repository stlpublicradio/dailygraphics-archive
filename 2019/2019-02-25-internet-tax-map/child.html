37 states have passed laws to collect sales tax from internet retailers, other remote sellers

A Missouri committee is considering whether the state should pass a law to collect sales tax from internet retailers. There are two types of laws that states use to tax remote sales, including internet purchases. Both require retailers to pay sales tax if revenue passes a threshold within that state. A retailer does not need to have a physical operation in that state to be taxed under the laws.

Notes

Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire and Oregon have no sales taxes. Approved Tax Law indicates states that have passed one of two types of laws that allow taxing of remote sellers; some of these states have voted to tax remote sellers but have not yet implemented the taxes.
