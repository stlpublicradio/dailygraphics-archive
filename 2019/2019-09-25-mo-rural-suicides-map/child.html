Rural counties had higher risk for suicide from 2014 to 2016

All counties in Missouri — and most in Illinois — have an increased risk of suicide, according to research by Ohio State University's Wexner Medical Center. The research compares the number of deaths by suicide from 2014-2016 with an expected number of suicides based on county populations and the national suicide rate to calculate a "standardized mortality ratio." Counties with ratios greater than one have a higher-than-projected risk of suicide. Stone county in southwest Missouri had one of the highest ratios in the country.

Source: Ohio State University Wexner Medical Center
